Flow is my attempt at creating a wearable ornament out of resin, metal, elastic thread, and glass beads. At the same time, I attempted to use a traditional frame to show the theme, which is an Asian conical hat to express romance. The resin solidifies the traditional construction by dripping down into raindrops that hang down from the hat's edge like a rain curtain. The resin's dance and movement is captured as it moves freely across the frame and finally solidifies in the metal. In reinterpreting tradition, I employ a new generation of materials. This demonstrates that precious metals and stones are no longer the mainstays of the jewelry industry; resin, acrylic, fabric, and other lighter and more malleable materials have made their appearance. These materials are more capable than a single metal of shaping the traditional ideal world.

I adore the ancient romantic fantasy of "clouds as hats and rain as curtains," which I see as a figurative representation of the ancient spiritual world. The myth is made up, but it is based on facts that people want to know; it is a piece of history that belongs to cultural memory. The heterogeneous world becomes more dramatic in its multiplicity of fiction, reality, and memory. It is a wandering of the mind, a spiritual energy required for modern life, without regard for reason, logic, or dependability. I believe that, after a long period of numbness, people desperately need a short break from this sober life to rediscover the liberation of romantic stories.

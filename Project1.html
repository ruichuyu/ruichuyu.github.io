Dreaming is one of the three states of the soul in life, and it is described as being intermediate between the waking and sleeping states in most ancient Asian texts. During sleep, the soul is divided into two parts: one that stays inside the body and the other that leaves the body and flutters around like a butterfly; thus, the butterfly is also considered a symbol of the human soul. Dreams are an extension of the capacity of the mind. In the dream world, the brain quickly constructs a world of crises and opportunities, and we instinctively and quickly choose a path to pursue or escape. This body of work is my interpretation of the dream world, which is in a state of flux while being constrained by our minds.

The ensemble is made of brass and watercolour paper, and it includes a skirt and a headpiece. The skirt has a distinct border, implying the entanglement and constraint of a dream world that traps us in our sleep like a cage. The intricate hollowing, on the other hand, makes the piece not very rigid, and the lightness of the paper reduces the weight of the metal. The extensive watercolour haloing creates a blur of color that is both dark and light in comparison to the underpainting on the paper. The shattered tin foil at the skirt's bottom reflects some of the surrounding badlands, implying that the dream world also maps reality. The multi-layered structure also implies that the dream world is a multi-dimensional space of transformation rather than just one layer.

An exploration of colour and texture: The Box Ring

Color haloing was investigated further in the Dream Interpretation project. I experimented with different mediums, including but not limited to watercolour, dye, and acrylic, to keep the original color of the colors and blur the boundaries of the different colors. Watercolour dyes gradually spread, resulting in granular borders. Watercolor and acrylic do not mix well. The texture of acrylics, on the other hand, can be shaped in ways that watercolour cannot, and this is how the Box Ring series came to be. This texture and color are created by squeezing large amounts of black and white acrylic paint; the more squeezing, the darker the color. At the same time, the texture becomes spiky rather than smooth and linear.
